Matcha

what is matcha?

Matcha contains high levels of antioxidants, particularly catechins, which are a type of flavonoid. Catechins can help fight free radicals in the body, reduce stress, and support overall cellular health. These antioxidants can help prevent cell damage and reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and cancer. The concentration of caffeine in matcha and the amino acid L-theanine have specific effects on the brain. L-theanine improves focus and reduces the negative effects of coffee, including anxiety. L-theanine and caffeine work together to improve memory, mental stamina, and cognitive function. Matcha helps cleanse the body naturally. Chlorophyll, the green pigment that gives tea leaves their color, can help remove heavy metals and other harmful substances from the body. This provides a detoxifying effect that is beneficial for kidney and liver health. The antioxidants contained in matcha are also good for skin health, helping to fight premature aging and acne.
